<Section icon={Swords} title="Yoru Moveset, Mastery, and Combat Timing">

Yoru’s kit is compact, which is part of why it feels so clean. Both skills feed into each other, and the mastery requirements are low enough that the sword becomes useful without a long grind.

| Move | Mastery | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Z - Dark Slash** | **50** | Thin vertical green slash with strong long-range pressure |
| **X - Thousand Slices** | **150** | Heavy multi-hit pressure with stun and Instinct Break value |

| Move Trait | Practical Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Long-range option | **Z** lets you threaten from safer spacing |
| Control option | **X** gives you a stun window to continue pressure |
| Chain potential | Both skills can combo into each other |
| Extra value | You can **M1 during the skill** on Thousand Slices |

<Callout type="info" title="What Makes the Kit Work">
The real strength of Yoru is not just raw damage. It is the way the sword creates a stable follow-up window after you force movement, stun, or hesitation.
</Callout>

**Move Breakdown**
- **Dark Slash** is your more direct opener. It is long-range and useful when you need to threaten a target before they settle into neutral.
- **Thousand Slices** is the more explosive tool. It can **Instinct Break**, stuns for about **0.75 seconds**, and gives you room to continue pressure.
- The low mastery requirements make the sword feel usable quickly, which is one reason it stays popular among players who like efficient progression.

| Combat Question | Best Answer |
