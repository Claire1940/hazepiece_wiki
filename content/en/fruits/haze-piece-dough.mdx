<Section icon={Shield} title="Best Combat Uses, Farming, and Route Planning">

Dough performs well when the fight has room to breathe. That is why it feels strong in boss pressure, group fights, and open-space PvP. You get more value from the fruit when you control distance and punish movement mistakes. It is less about raw speed and more about making the enemy move where you want them to go.

<Callout type="tip" title="Best Use Cases">
If you want one fruit that supports travel, pressure, and boss damage at the same time, Dough is a safe long-term pick.
</Callout>

| Mode | Performance | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| PvE Grinding | **Good** | Clear burst and usable range |
| Boss Fighting | **Strong** | Grab windows create safe damage |
| PvP Pressure | **Strong** | Stun, launch, and follow-up tools |
| Map Travel | **Very Strong** | Water Walk and Rolling Doughnut improve routing |

A clean route for haze piece dough users looks like this:

1. Move into range with Rolling Doughnut.
2. Tag the enemy with a poke.
3. Lock them with a control move.
4. Spend your strongest follow-up.
5. Reset before the enemy recovers.

That pattern is especially useful when enemies try to retreat or when a boss is on a predictable cooldown. You do not need to overcommit every time. In fact, holding one move back often gives you better uptime across a long fight.
