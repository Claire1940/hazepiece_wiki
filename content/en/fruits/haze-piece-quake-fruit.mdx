<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat Route for Farming, Bossing, and PvP">

The cleanest Quake Fruit route is simple: group first, strike second, reset third. That pattern keeps your damage efficient and avoids the classic mistake of chasing every hit when the enemy is already moving out of range.

<Callout type="tip" title="Best Combat Pattern">
Open with area pressure, wait for the enemy to commit, and then use your biggest burst window. Quake Fruit rewards patience more than panic.
</Callout>

| Situation | What to Do | What to Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Mob farming | Pull enemies together before casting | Scattering packs with sloppy movement |
| Bossing | Save burst for recovery animations | Wasting cooldowns during invulnerability |
| PvP | Force movement into your hit zone | Chasing too far into counterpressure |
| Team fights | Hold space with area attacks | Tunneling one target while others collapse |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set the battlefield">
    Move enemies into a small area before you commit your main damage. Quake Fruit gets much stronger when targets stay clustered.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Spend burst at the right moment">
    Use your strongest hit after the enemy has already committed. That is where quake-style damage usually feels most efficient.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reset safely">
    After the combo, step back and re-center. Do not stand inside the enemy's retaliation window just to squeeze in one extra hit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Common Mistake | Better Choice | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Overcommitting into melee range | Cast from a safe angle | Less damage taken |
| Using burst too early | Wait for recovery frames | Better hit confirmation |
| Ignoring movement tools | Add repositioning support | Cleaner escapes |
| Fighting isolated targets inefficiently | Drag enemies together | Higher damage value |

</Section>
