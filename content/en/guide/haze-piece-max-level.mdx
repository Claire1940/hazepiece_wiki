<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Build Choices While Leveling">

Your build decides whether leveling feels smooth or painful. Fruit builds are best when your fruit clears groups quickly, Sword builds are best when your weapon access is strong, and Fighting Style builds are best when you want a simple melee lane with reliable damage.

<Callout type="success" title="Build Rule">
Keep **Defense** as your second priority in most cases. Spreading points across every lane usually slows the climb.
</Callout>

| Build | Stat Priority | Best Early Tools | When to Swap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fruit | Fruit > Defense | Flame, Ice, Light, Shadow | Swap when your clear speed stops matching quest density |
| Sword | Sword > Defense | Shark Blade, Shusui, Gryphon | Swap when mastery growth feels slower than quest gains |
| Fighting Style | Style/Combat > Defense | Black Leg, Electro, Fishman | Swap when reach or AoE starts limiting your farming |

Haki is part of the same leveling picture, because it reduces friction while you grind.

| Haki | Unlock Point | Value While Leveling |
|---|---|---|
| Flash Step | Level 40 | Faster travel, safer pulls, cleaner routeing |
| Buso Haki | Level 350 | Better weapon and style damage |
| Observation Haki | Level 600 | Dodges, safer bossing, better survival |

Use the Haki table as a checkpoint list. If your build is strong but your movement tools are lagging behind, the run will still feel slow. The best leveling characters usually combine one main damage lane with the next unlocked utility tool.
