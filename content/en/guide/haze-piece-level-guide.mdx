<Section icon={Target} title="Best Leveling Loop and Quest Order">

The core loop is simple: take the current island quest, clear the required enemies, turn it in, then move to the next practical route. That method keeps EXP per minute steady and avoids dead travel time.

<Callout type="warning" title="Route Discipline">
Do not free-roam for too long. Side fights, long detours, and unplanned boss hunting slow your level curve more than weak gear does.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Redeem boosts and set your route">
    Claim active codes first, then decide whether you are leveling with a Fruit, Sword, or Fighting Style. Use the same path for the full session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Run only the current island quest">
    Pick the mission that matches your level range. Quest loops usually outpace random enemy farming because they also pay cash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Move when the next gate opens">
    As soon as a new island or trainer becomes realistic, switch routes instead of overfarming an easy zone.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Add bosses only when they fit the loop">
    Bosses are great for drops and bonus rewards, but they should support your route, not replace it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Level Band | Best Activity | Why It Works |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1-40 | Starter quests, codes, Flash Step setup | Fast first levels and less travel friction |
| 40-350 | Island quest chain | Stable EXP and cash with low risk |
| 350-600 | Buso Haki route, stronger quests | Better damage and safer fights |
| 600+ | Observation Haki route, boss assists | Higher survivability and better late-game utility |

<Callout type="info" title="Routing Tip">
If a quest chain is efficient, keep it. A slightly weaker fight with better turn-in speed is often better than a flashy detour.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Stat Builds, Fruits, and Weapon Loadouts">
